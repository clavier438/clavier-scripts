# Distribution — community virality is the whole game

A product on Gumroad with no traffic sells ~0. Building it was the easy 20%;
getting it in front of the right people is the 80%. **The means is always
community virality** — getting the tool shared in the communities where its users
already hang out. Don't wait for Gumroad's marketplace to find you.

## The principle

Always plan the viral angle before (or alongside) the listing. For every product
answer: *where do its users already gather, and what story makes them share it?*
Then show up as a real participant, not a marketer.

## How to post so it spreads (not flagged as spam)

- **Lead with the story and the problem, not the link.** "I kept needing X, the
  paid tools were Y, so I built this" — the maker's-bench honesty is exactly what
  these communities reward. The sale is a footnote.
- **Give value first.** Share the idea/approach freely; the cheap paid version is
  for people who'd rather not build it themselves. "Here's how it works + a $7
  version if you want it done" goes over well.
- **Be a real account.** Post from somewhere with history, reply to comments,
  don't drive-by drop links. One genuine Show HN beats ten spammy crossposts.
- **Show, don't tell.** A 10-second gif/screenshot of the tool working converts
  far better than prose.

## The "why pay vs free" hook (every product needs one)

There's always a free alternative, so name the reason to pay:
- **pdf-to-jpeg** → privacy/offline: "your PDFs never leave your Mac" (vs sketchy upload sites)
- **web-site-exporter / site-scraper** → batch + whole-site, no per-page clicking, no quotas
- **img-to-web** → batch + scriptable, not one-at-a-time drag tools
- **all of them** → it's a script you own and can change, no subscription, no account

## Measure, then double down

Listing is cheap, so seed several — but put real effort into ONE post for the most
promising, see if it converts, and let the data tell you which tool to push.
Whatever gets traction, go deeper there: more posts, and an SEO landing page in
`landing/` targeting that search term. Don't spread effort evenly across losers.
