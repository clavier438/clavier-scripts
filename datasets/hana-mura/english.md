LOCAL EXPERIENCE LOCAL EXPERIENCE Yamanaka Onsen, Kaga, Ishikawa, Japan Yamanaka Onsen, Kaga, Ishikawa, Japan The Mountains that live forever and gentle valleys. Purple clouds and murmuring streams, tinted by the changing light. In the midst of nature that produces unparalleled scenery, we convey the comfort that has been passed down from generation to generation, as well as new culture. LOCATION Hanamurasaki is a traditional Japanese inn but it has unique style. Hanamurasaki at Yamanaka Onsen is in a location which is almost the same distance from major cities such as Tokyo, Osaka, Kyoto, and Nagoya, moreover is very easily accessible and just about 30 min by car(taxi) from Komatsu Airport. CUISINE We serve dishes imbued with the climate of Ishikawa. Our chic seasonal menu is available as a kaiseki course meal, or a la carte so guests can choose according to their tastes and dietary restrictions. We offer beverage pairings including sake, wine, beer, and tea. This dining experience is rooted in the traditions and philosophy of Japanese cuisine, while incorporating contemporary creativity. MORE GUEST ROOMS From every room, you can see Kakusenkei gorge. The grandeur of nature and a sense of serenity that can only be found in Yamanaka Onsen flows through the guest rooms. We encourage guests to relax their bodies and minds in the healing space, where ancient elegance and modern comfort are woven together. MORE SABO Our sabo, or tea room, is a special place where guests can peacefully commune with themselves and others. We offer a selection of teas, including sencha, Japanese black tea, Kaga bōcha (a local variety of roasted twig tea), and even tea cocktails, all served using one-of-a-kind tea utensils created in collaboration with artists. We hope to create a moment of connection through our unique modern style of tea experience. MORE ONSEN Yamanaka onsen is natural hot spring from boasting more than 1300 years history. Feel healing by the high quality hot spring. The onsen is not only great relaxation but also good for health and skin, giving the skin a silky and smooth texture. LOCAL EXPERIENCE At Hanamurasaki, we offer exclusive tours that are deeply rooted in the local culture. Immerse yourself in the natural beauty, traditional crafts, and unique art that this land has to offer. MORE OUR STORY Hanamurasaki traces its roots back to 1902, when its predecessor, Yamadaya, first opened its doors. At the time, inns with natural hot springs were few and far between, and Yamadaya soon became a cherished retreat for travellers. In 1984, inspired by the gentle beauty of the surrounding mountain valley, the owner of the previous generation established Hanamurasaki. Its name comes from the poetic phrase Sanshi Suimei – “purple mountains and clear waters” – an expression that captures the fleeting, one-of-a-kind beauty found in nature. Today, Hanamurasaki carries this spirit forward. We continue to explore what it means to share Japanese culture in a contemporary context, with the hope of quietly passing it on to those who come next. A Check-in is from 2:00pm. and check-out is at 11:00am. Q How late can I check in? What should I do if I will be checking in late? A Please note that if we do not hear from you, we may consider your reservation cancelled as of 6:00pm of the date reserved. Q Can you hold my luggage before I check in or after I check out? A We'll be pleased to take care of your luggage at the front desk. Q Is there free parking lot for guests? A Yes, there is. Please park in front of the entrance. Our staff will move your car to the parking lot. Q Is there a free shuttle for guests? A We offer free shuttle service from Kagaonsen station between 12pm and 6pm, by reservation. If you would like to take the shuttle, please let us know via email your arrival time and train name at Kagaonsen station one day in advance of your stay. The return shuttle to Kagaonsen is available from 9:00am to 11:30am. Q Is there free shuttle from Komatsu Airport? A Unfortunately, no, there isn’t. Free shuttle is from Kagaonsen station only. Please take a taxi from Komatsu Airport. Q Are there any open-air bath with rooms? A Yes, there are 3 comfort suite rooms and 2 Jr.suite rooms installed open-air bath. Q Are there non-smoking rooms? A Yes, all rooms are non-smoking rooms. Q Are there bed rooms? A Yes, Comfort suite rooms and Jr.suite rooms are bed rooms. Other room types are Japanese futon bedding but we could provide rollaway bed on request as well. Q Is there a nicer view room? A All rooms are facing the river and have nice view. Q I have a food allergy. A It corresponds to the extent possible If you will tell us in advance via email. Q When should I choose a la carte kaiseki menu? A A la carte menu is in your room. Please choose by dinner. Q Is it possible to serve a la carte kaiseki in room dining? A It is not possible. A la carte kaiseki can be served dining restaurant only. Q Is it possible to have meals with under 10years old child at the dining restaurant. A It is not possible to enter with under 10yeards old child to the dining restaurant. If you stay with under 10years old child, please choose in-room dining plan. Q What time do you serve dinner and breakfast? A It starts serving from 6pm to 7pm for dinner, and 7:30am to 9:00am for breakfast. Q Is free Wi-Fi internet access available? A We have free Wi-Fi in the room and the lobby. Q Which credit cards do you accept? A We accept the following cards: VISA, JCB, AMEX, Diners, UC, DC, NICOS, Master Card, AEON, LIFE, ORICO, 銀聯. Q Can I request a massage in my room? A Massages are available in your room. The massages are sometimes crowded, so it's better to make a reservation before your stay via email. Q Can I send a package from Hanamurasaki to another hotel? A We accept only domestic shipping. We do not accept international shipping from here. Q Is it wheelchair accessible? A It is accessible in the facility and we provide complimentary usage of wheelchairs within the ryokan. There are 2 accessible Japanese western comfort suite rooms with wheelchair accessible a private hot spring bath. It is not accessible for traditional Japanese rooms. Q Can I bring some drinks during dinner and breakfast? A We do not accept drink from outside during dinner and breakfast. It is fine to have drinks in your room. Q Do you provide pajamas? A All rooms are provided with traditional yukata robes as pajamas. Q How long does it take to Yuge kaido(main street of Yamanaka town) by walk? Can you take me there by car? A It takes about 15min by walk. We do not offer taking a guest there. We offer free shuttle from Kaganonsen station only. Q Do you offer a day trip bathing. A We do not offer a day trip bathing. It is for staying guests only. Q Do you open for lunch? A We offer Japanese style Afternoon tea for lunch reservation needed. Q Can I make a reservation on the day I wish to arrive? A Yes, please make a reservation through our official web site. Q What are your cancellation charges? A The following charges will apply if you cancel your reservations.
