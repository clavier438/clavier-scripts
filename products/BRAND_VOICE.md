# Common spirit — the maker's bench

The unifying voice for every tool sold under this line. Read this before writing any
product copy. The point: a buyer should feel they're getting a sharp tool off a real
maker's bench — not a SaaS landing page.

## The ethos (one line)

> Sharp little tools I built for my own work, the hard way — now yours, cheap and open,
> to take apart and rebuild around how *you* work.

## What we believe

- **Built, not bought.** Each tool exists because the commercial option was overpriced,
  bloated, or locked-down. I needed it to actually work, so I made it. It's battle-tested
  on my own real work — not a demo.
- **Cheap on purpose.** Priced low so you just grab it and use it. No subscription,
  no account, no lock-in.
- **Yours to hack.** It's a readable script, not a sealed black box. Open it, change it,
  bend it to your own workflow.
- **Honest.** No support desk, no roadmap theater. It's the real thing I use, shipped
  as-is, and it does one job well.

## Voice

- First person, plain, a little blunt — like a maker handing you a tool off their bench.
- No marketing fluff. Banned words: revolutionize, seamless, empower, unleash, game-changer.
- Confident, not corporate. Short sentences. Lead with what it does and why it's better.
- One honest heads-up near the end (it's a script, not a polished SaaS).
- English copy. Lowercase, calm, specific.

## Naming

Each product: a plain descriptive name + the same em-dash tagline shape, e.g.
"WebSiteExporter — Full-page website to PDF". Say what it does in the name. No cute brands.
